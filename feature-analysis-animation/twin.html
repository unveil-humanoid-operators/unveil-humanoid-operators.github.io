Same robot. Same motion. Different operator — and the motion still remembers them.

Two pairs of side-by-side viewers show the same retargeted clip driving identical Unitree G1 robots while a SMPL body above each robot renders the operator's true shape. Retargeting strips body shape from the robot, yet the robot's motion still encodes the operator: a heavier operator's root translation runs slower (jumping, left), and an older operator's waist range-of-motion is narrower (turning, right).

turning  ·  age × waist ROM
Each panel shows the same retargeted G1 trajectory driving both robots; the visible difference is synthesized to illustrate the population-level partial-correlation finding (heavy robot's root excursions × 0.80 for jumping; older operator's waist joints × 0.55 for turning).
